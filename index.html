Introduction to
Mechanical Engineering

Mechanical engineering is one of the oldest and broadest engineering disciplines. It combines principles of physics and materials science for the design, analysis, manufacturing, and maintenance of mechanical systems.

Modern CAD Design Process in Mechanical Engineering

Core Principles

At its core, mechanical engineering involves understanding and applying fundamental principles such as:

Mechanics
The study of forces and their effects on matter, including statics (forces in equilibrium), dynamics (forces causing motion), and mechanics of materials (how materials respond to forces).
Thermodynamics
The study of energy, heat transfer, and the relationships between heat and work, critical for designing engines, refrigeration systems, and power plants.
Materials Science
Understanding the properties and behaviors of materials used in mechanical systems, and how to select appropriate materials for specific applications.
Design
The process of creating systems, components, or processes that meet desired needs within constraints such as economics, environmental impact, safety, and manufacturability.

Applications

Mechanical engineers work across various industries, including:

Automotive Design and Manufacturing
Aerospace Systems
Robotics and Automation
Energy and Power Generation
